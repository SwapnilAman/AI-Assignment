Problem – Solve 8-queens problem using Uniform Cost Search.

The State Space of the given problem is as follows.

1.)	Initial State – Initially we assume that there are no queens on the board at all which means that the entire board is empty.

2.)	States – The relative arrangement of the 8- queens on the chessboard denotes the respective states of the problem.

3.)	Action – Starting from left place the queens on the empty squares of the chessboards such that it is not attacked by any other queen, either horizontally, vertically or diagonally.

4.)	Cost – A cost of one unit per move is incurred.

5.)	Goal state – All the 8 queens should be arranged on the chess board such that no two queens attack each other.
